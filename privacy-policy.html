Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what information the Resurface browser extension collects and how it is used.

1. What we store

When you save a page, Resurface stores the page's URL, title, and favicon locally on your device using Chrome's built-in storage APIs. This data stays in your browser and is not transmitted to us or any third-party server.

2. What we don't collect

We do not track your browsing history, collect analytics on the pages you save, or sell any data to third parties. Resurface does not include advertising or ad-tracking of any kind.

3. Payment information

If you subscribe to the paid plan, your payment is processed entirely by our payment processor, Paddle.com. We do not receive or store your card details. Paddle may collect billing information (such as email and payment method) as needed to process your subscription, governed by Paddle's own Privacy Policy.

4. Subscription status

To verify an active subscription, the extension checks a license key against our payment processor's license verification service. This check only confirms whether a key is valid; it does not share the contents of your saved pages.

5. Data deletion

Uninstalling the extension removes all locally stored data. Since we do not keep a copy of your saved pages on our own servers, there is nothing further for us to delete on request beyond your subscription/billing record, which Paddle manages.

6. Children's privacy

The Service is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect information from children.

7. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by an updated "Last updated" date above.

8. Contact
